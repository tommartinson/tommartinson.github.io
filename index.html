Projects

Board Game Simulation

This program using the Python agent-based modeling library, Mesa, simulates a simple randomized board game. Two teams, red and blue, seek to find and capture each other on a board. Each piece starts on a random, independent square. Teams take turns moving 1 adjacent space in a random direction each move. If they land on a space already occupied by an opponent, they capture the piece. The first team to capture all of the other pieces wins.

Lanchester's Law Simulation

This program simulates a demonstration of Lanchester's Square Law using the Mesa Python library. Lanchester's Law is a differential equation describing the time dependence of two armies' strengths, α and β, as a function of time, with the function only depending on α and β. Lanchester's Law has been used to model historical battles for research purposes. Examples include Pickett's Charge of Confederate infantry against Union infantry during the 1863 Battle of Gettysburg, and the 1940 Battle of Britain between the British and German air forces.

Diet & Exercise Model

A simple model of a board game whereby rivals (red vs. blue) seek to find and capture each other in a geographical region of numbered circles. They start on random independent circles, and take turns moving 1 adjacent circle in a random direction each move. If they land on a circle where their rival lies, they capture the other and win.
